The minimum wage will increase by 28 percent, far from the expectations of the unions

Moroni Source: Archive

Santiago Dapelo

october 12, 2020 • 3:24 pm

Action Anoth Some

In the midst of the economic crisis, the living and mobile minimum wage will increase 28 percent. As confirmed by official sources, it was decided that it be done in three sections: 12%, in October; 10%, in December, and 6%, in March. In addition, it was defined that the Salary Council will meet again in April to evaluate inflationary evolution.

Thus, the minimum wage will go from $ 16,875 to $ 21,600, far from the expectation that some unions had, which asked for an increase of 40%, closer to the inflation estimated for this year than in September it already adds 22.3%. The agreement that was closed in plenary session of the Employment Council was attended by the Minister of Labor,Claudio Moroni, the leadership of the CGT, the two CTAs and the main business chambers.

The agreement, which was by a majority - for the first time in three years the Executive Power did not have to laud - left a little appreciated also among the business chambers, which in the middle of a year that had a very strong drop in activity, offered a 20 percent increase.

The possibility of discussing a new increase in six months was what made it possible for the unions to accept the figure that puts the minimum wage below inflation.

The definition of the new salary floor will impact some 400,000 workers outside the agreement and is important because it serves as a reference for the initial teacher salary and social plans, since half of the amount represents the complementary social salary. In addition, the minimum wage is an indicator for informal work and for activities that are not unionized in eight-hour work days.

The maximum objective that the unionists had set was to bring the minimum wage to the value of the basic basket, which today is estimated at $ 46,800. Hours before the meeting, the CGT expressed their intention to request an increase of between 35 and 40 percent. "I do not know if the 36 or 40%, but the proposal of the Salary Council has to be around," said Héctor Daer in AM750.

A similar message was expressed by the leader of the Workers' CTA, Hugo Yasky: "The expectation is to try to recover the Minimum, Vital and Mobile Salary that is currently $ 16,875." And he added: "We are going to try to get as close as possible to the Basic Basket."

A similar message was expressed by the leader of the Workers' CTA, Hugo Yasky: "The expectation is to try to recover the Minimum, Vital and Mobile Salary that is currently $ 16,875." And he added: "We are going to try to get as close as possible to the Basic Basket."

The only ones who were dissatisfied with the arrangement were the representatives of the Autonomous CTA, who demanded a much higher raise.

In addition to the unions, the 16 representatives of the business chambers were present, such as the Argentine Industrial Union (UIA), the Rural Society and the Argentine Chamber of Commerce. The meeting, which was virtual, lasted almost four hours.

Thus, the national government formalized the first increase in the minimum wage. The last increase had been ordered in September of last year by the then Minister of Production and Labor Dante Sica, who, in the absence of an agreement between businessmen and union members, decided to raise the salary floor from $ 12,500 to $ 16,875.

En el encuentro, que fue encabezado por Moroni, también estuvieron presentes Santiago Cafiero, Jefe de Gabinete, y Daniel Arroyo, Ministro de Desarrollo Social. En representación del sector sindical, participaron Héctor Daer, Julio Piumato, Víctor Santa María, Jorge Solá, Noemí Ruiz, Antonio Calo, Jorge Carlos Acuña, Andrés Rodríguez, Gerardo Martínez, Sergio Romero y José Luis Lingieri, por parte de la Confederación General del Trabajo (CGT); y Hugo Yasky y Ricardo Peidro, de la Central de Trabajadores de la Argentina (CTA).

Meanwhile, the businessmen were present Daniel Funes from Rioja, José Etala, Guillermo Moretti and Horacio Martínez from the Argentine Industrial Union (UIA); Pedro Etcheberry, representing the Argentine Chamber of Commerce and Services (CAC); Jorge Hulton, from the Argentine Chamber of Construction (CAMARCO); Andrés Dominguez of the Argentine Agrarian Federation (FAA); Paulo Ares, from the Confederación Intercooperativa Agropecuaria Limitada (CONINAGRO); Alberto Froia, on behalf of the Argentine Rural Confederations (CRA); Guillermo Gambie, from the Buenos Aires Stock Exchange (BCBA); Pedro Cascales, from the Argentine Confederation of Medium Enterprises (CAME); and Juan Carlos Ciolli on behalf of Small and Medium Enterprises (PYMES).
